  • Earth movement

    Planet Earth performs two main movements. In a first movement, called a translation, it traces an orbit around the Sun. It also performs a rotation on its own axis, called a rotation. Each translation around the Sun represents a year, while each rotation around its own axis defines a day.

  • The moon

    The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It was formed at practically the same time as the Earth with the emergence of the Universe. Because of its proximity to planet Earth, it is the largest and brightest object in the terrestrial night sky. Being the fifth largest moon in the solar system.

  • Galileo Galilei

    Galileo Galilei was an important Italian astronomer, physicist and mathematician. It is considered a landmark of the scientific revolution in the fields of physics and astronomy. Galileo's studies were fundamental for the development of mechanics (the movement of bodies) and the discovery of planets and satellites.

  • Solar System

    The Solar System is a set of celestial bodies that gravitate in the orbit of a sun (a star). Our solar system is made up of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

  • Astronomical Units

    Astronomical unit is a unit of length given by the average distance from the Earth to the Sun, equivalent to 149.6 x 106. The Earth-Sun distance varies throughout the year, as the Earth's orbit around the Sun has a slight eccentricity: during apogee and perigee, the Earth is at maximum and minimum distances, respectively, from the Sun.

  • Light-years

    Light year is a unit of measurement used in astronomy. It means the distance that a photon (a particle of light) travels in a year in a vacuum. A light year is the distance traveled by light in one year in a vacuum.
