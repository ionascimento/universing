Astronomical Units

Astronomical unit is a unit of length given by the average distance from the Earth to the Sun, equivalent to 149.6 x 106. The Earth-Sun distance varies throughout the year, as the Earth's orbit around the Sun has a slight eccentricity: during apogee and perigee, the Earth is at maximum and minimum distances, respectively, from the Sun.
